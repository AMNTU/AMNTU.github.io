How much help do the world's poorest countries need?

  • Introduction
  • Population
  • Economics
  • Education
  • Conclusion
A large population of people in the poorest countries are living without basic life essentials and infrastructure, such as electricity and sanitation. Obviously, investments in education are also very low in these countries. To get out of the vicious poverty cycle, the education level must reach a state where the productivity capacity of the people is increased adequately to support and accelerate economic growth. It has been highlighted in the 1990 World Development Report that the most effective way to help the poor is by (1) leveraging their labour for economic growth, (2) investing in their education and healthcare and (3) providing well-targeted safety nets.
